Limitations


I have had many limitations throughout this process. One limitation I have had since the beginning of this project was being sure about the processes. I have had to teach myself the data collection software, learned how to read the predictability charts, and have had to guess many times when it comes to nuances or fine print that no one would usually think of specifying, that could play a large role. For instance, pressing start before the schedule file runs, or trusting that it will do it itself. I have also had to troubleshoot many things, including the conductivity of my antenna and how that changes with prolonged exposure to the elements. With a radio telescope being an electrical machine, things are also prone to break. I have had to replace my receiver, and teach myself the new software that came along with it. I have used a raspberry pi, radio skypipe, and RSS to try and collect data, and each software has come with a host of learning curves and, of course, problems. Other mistakes include little errors that proved larger due to the remote location of my telescope and how hard it is to get out there – If my charger is not turned on correctly, then there goes another possible data point and the next time there could be another is in 10 days…oops. As I continued to troubleshoot, I also ran into issues surrounding the time of Jupiter being in the beam of my antenna continuously moving earlier into the day, in which it is blocked by the more intense noise of the sun. I also think my original hypothesis was not as well thought out as it could have been, and that the purpose of doing this final research has morphed into something else, which alright in technicality and honestly the purpose of research, but it still leaves a feeling of purposelessness within the project.
